# Equity Delivery Trading Guide

Equity Delivery is when you buy stocks and hold them for more than one day. The shares are credited to your Demat account, and you become a part-owner of the company.

## Charge Breakdown

### 1. Brokerage
- **Rate**: Zero (0). Zerodha does not charge any brokerage for delivery trades.
- This is one of the biggest advantages for long-term investors.

### 2. Securities Transaction Tax (STT)
- **Rate**: 0.1% on both Buy and Sell sides.
- **Note**: This is significantly higher than intraday STT.

### 3. Transaction Charges
- **Rate**: 0.00345% on both sides.
- These are exchange-specific and vary slightly between NSE and BSE.

### 4. GST
- **Rate**: 18% on (Brokerage + Transaction Charges). Since brokerage is zero, GST is only applied to transaction charges.

### 5. SEBI Charges
- **Rate**: Rs. 10 per crore.

### 6. Stamp Duty
- **Rate**: 0.015% or Rs. 1500 per crore on the Buy side only.

## Mathematical Formula

The total charges (C) can be calculated as:
`C = 0 + STT + T + GST + SEBI + SD`

Where:
- `STT = 0.001 * (buy_value + sell_value)`
- `T = 0.0000345 * total_turnover`
- `GST = 0.18 * T`
- `SEBI = 0.0000001 * total_turnover`
- `SD = 0.00015 * buy_value`

## Long-term Investment Tips
- Delivery is ideal for wealth creation over years.
- Dividends are credited directly to your bank account linked with the Demat.
- Avoid frequent trading in delivery to minimize the impact of STT.
